§ 2-600. How to Cite Court Rules [ BB | ALWD ]

Principle: Rules of evidence or procedure are cited by name of the set of rules (beginning with the jurisdiction) «e.g.»

  • Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6).
  • Fed. R. Crim. P. 7(b).
  • Haw. Fam. Ct. R. 106.
  • N.J. Ct. R. 3:8-3.
and the rule number «e.g.».
  • Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6) .
  • Fed. R. Crim. P. 7(b) .
  • Haw. Fam. Ct. R. 106 .
  • N.J. Ct. R. 3:8-3 .
The name is abbreviated. According to The Bluebook, the ALWD Guide to Legal Citation , and widespread practice, no date need be included so long as the citation's reference is to the rule currently in effect.
